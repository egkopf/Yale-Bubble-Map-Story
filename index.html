Kopf Bubble Map Outline v2

The story that I aim to tell begins with asking the reader to consider the concept of a quick stop for coffee or a snack before or after a class. With what frequency do Yale students choose to go to privately-owned, non-Yale coffee shops, where they may be surrounded by non-Yale-affiliated residents of New Haven, and served by non-Yale-employed people? And conversely, to what degree has Yale’s establishment of University coffee shops drawn students into Yale spaces who would have otherwise chosen to get their coffee or snack at a non-Yale space?

Beat 1: New Haven is a town in CT

Map of the top-10 Yale buildings by student class attendance. This map should demonstrate geographic area within New Haven where students are most frequently found during the school day. Intention is to familiarize the reader with the traffic patterns of students across Yale and non-Yale spaces.

Beat 2: Yale University is located smack dab in the middle of New Haven

Homing in on New Haven in 2011, before the opening of Steep Café, the Silliman Acorn, or the Elm. This map will highlight the proximity of classroom buildings to commercial areas in New Haven. It is not necessarily meant to claim that students commonly patronized independent organizations for coffee and snacks before and after classes, but rather to demonstrate the time before students were in the habit of relying on Yale spaces for these activities.
